Melissa Jo Carson

PhD Candidate · Department of Applied Economics · University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

I am a third-year PhD student in Applied Economics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. I am advised by Professor Andrew Hultgren. My economic research intersects socio-environmental justice, climate change, and human health.

I pursue two main research streams:

(1) estimating how climate change exacerbates heterogeneous distributional impacts of environmental damages and inequity; (2) understanding the role of environmental litigation itself on firm behavior.

My academic values are: curiosity, collegiality, rigor, interdisciplinary, inclusion, and transparency. I am equi-part passionate about academic research, teaching, and mentorship.

Please feel welcome to drop me an email if our paths cross!
