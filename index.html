Melissa Jo Carson

PhD Candidate · Department of Applied Economics · UIUC

Hi there, I am Melissa.

A third-year PhD student in Applied Economics at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, advised by Professor Andrew Hultgren. My economic research intersects socio-environmental justice, climate change, and human health. Before graduate school, I supported economists with large-scale civil litigation damage calculations.

Currently, I pursue two main research streams: (1) estimating the heterogeneous distributional environmental inequity from climate change; (2) understanding the effectiveness of environmental litigation on firm behavior.

My academic values are curiosity, collegiality, rigor, interdisciplinarity, and transparency. I am equally passionate about academic research and mentorship.

Please send me an email if our paths cross or our research interests align!
