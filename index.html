Working Papers

Crop Insurance Subsidies and Technology Adoption Among Smallholder Farmers Job Market Paper

Jane A. Smith · 2024

This paper examines how access to subsidized crop insurance affects the adoption of improved seed varieties and fertilizer use among smallholder farmers in rural Kenya. Using a regression discontinuity design exploiting a program eligibility threshold, I find that insurance access increases fertilizer expenditure by 18% and adoption of improved seeds by 12 percentage points. The results suggest that credit constraints and risk aversion interact to suppress technology adoption.

Weather Shocks, Consumption Smoothing, and Informal Insurance Networks

Jane A. Smith and Robert J. Lee · 2023

We document how rural households in Ethiopia smooth consumption in the face of idiosyncratic and aggregate rainfall shocks, using 15 years of longitudinal panel data. Households with denser social networks exhibit significantly higher consumption insurance against aggregate shocks, consistent with informal risk-sharing models. We find that mobile money adoption weakens these informal networks for idiosyncratic shocks while leaving aggregate-shock insurance unchanged.

Land Tenure Security and Investment Incentives: Evidence from Uganda Published

Jane A. Smith · Journal of Development Economics, 2022

I exploit a land titling program in Uganda to estimate the causal effect of tenure security on long-term agricultural investment. Farmers who received formal land titles increased tree planting by 23% and soil conservation measures by 15%, consistent with Besley (1995). Effects are concentrated among female-headed households and those near urban markets.
